The Ultimate Carry‑On Guide for Stress‑Free Flights

Published Feb 9, 2025 • 7 min read

Traveling with only a carry‑on is freedom: no baggage carousels, no lost bags, and faster exits. This guide condenses what our support team sees travelers ask every day—what actually goes inside the bag, and how to avoid last‑minute repacking at security.

1) Start with airline rules

Dimensions and weight vary by airline. On Travelo’s checkout, your fare’s cabin bag limits appear next to the price—screenshot them. If you’re connecting airlines, follow the strictest set.

2) Pack by zones

  • Top pocket: liquids bag, passport, boarding pass.
  • Main zone: clothes in packing cubes (roll, don’t fold).
  • Front sleeve: laptop/tablet with an easy pull‑out.

3) The 3‑pouch rule

One pouch for tech (chargers, adapters), one for health (meds, sanitizer), one for comfort (eye mask, earplugs). They jump from bag to bag without rethinking.

4) Liquids & security

Follow your airport’s rules for liquids (often 100ml limit) and pack gels in a transparent zip bag. Keep it reachable to avoid rummaging.

5) Wear your bulkiest items

Jackets and sneakers eat space. Wear them, then stash mid‑flight if you want.

Checklist

  • Passport + visas
  • Universal adapter + phone cable
  • Earplugs + eye mask
  • Toiletries (travel size)
  • One extra outfit
