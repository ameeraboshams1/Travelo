Hotel Breakfasts: When to Skip and When to Splurge

Published May 29, 2025 • 4 min read

Breakfast can anchor your travel day—or drain your budget and time. Here’s how to decide when the hotel buffet is worth it and when a local café wins.

What a good hotel breakfast buys you

Time: Zero commute, fast start, consistent hours.
Certainty: Reliable options for picky eaters or dietary needs.

When to splurge on the buffet

  • Early tours/flights: 6–7 AM opening times save you a detour.
  • Remote areas: Few cafés nearby or nothing opens early.
  • Kids in tow: All-you-can-eat fruit, cereal, eggs—simple and quick.

When to skip it

  • You’re in a food city—half the fun is a neighborhood bakery.
  • Price is steep vs. your appetite (light eaters rarely get value).
  • You want flexibility: coffee now, proper brunch later.
Small win: If breakfast is optional at booking, compare price vs. a nearby café’s typical bill. Add the value of saved time before morning tours.

Hybrid approach

Book the first morning with the buffet to settle in, then switch to local spots. On Travelo, look for “Breakfast included” vs “Room only” during checkout—sometimes the price gap is tiny.
