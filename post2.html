How to Find Fair Flight Prices (Without Spending Hours)

Published Mar 14, 2025 • 6 min read

Great prices aren’t “luck”—they’re a few habits you can reuse for every trip. Here’s the fastest playbook our team uses inside Travelo to land honest fares without diving into dozens of tabs.

1) Start with flexible dates (±3)

Move your search window three days earlier/later. Tuesdays aren’t magically cheaper, but midweek demand often is. In Travelo’s calendar, look for the light-purple cells—those are your best snapshots.

2) Use nearby airports

Big hubs add capacity and competition. Search your city + alternatives within 100–200 km. A cheap train or rideshare can be worth a $120–$200 fare drop.

3) Mix & match airlines

One airline out, another back. Multi-carrier trips are often the hidden sweet spot, especially on long-hauls with open-jaw returns.

Pro tip: If you can travel light, filter “carry-on only” to surface ultra-competitive fares that don’t include checked bags.

4) Watch total trip cost, not just the ticket

  • Bag fees and seat fees (especially for families)
  • Transportation to/from alternate airports
  • Overnight connections that need a hotel

5) Set a price alert, then stop checking

Set alerts and let the system watch fares for you. The goal is to spend less time, not more. When the rate crosses your threshold, book with confidence.

6) The 24-hour rethink

Many regions offer a cooling-off window. If the price moves or plans change, you can sometimes adjust with minimal cost. You’ll see this clearly on Travelo during checkout.

Quick checklist

  • Try ±3 days and alternate airports
  • Compare cabin-bag vs. checked-bag fares
  • Consider open-jaw or multi-city
  • Turn on alerts and move on with your day
