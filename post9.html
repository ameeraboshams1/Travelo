Sports Gear & Instruments: Fly Without Headaches

Published Mar 3, 2025 • 7 min read

Skis, guitars, surfboards, cellos—special items can travel smoothly if you plan for the fees, dimensions, and handling ahead of time.

1) Measure and photograph

Note length × width × height and weight. Take a photo with a tape measure—handy if an agent asks.

2) Call the airline (yes, really)

Most carriers require advance notice for oversized or fragile items. Ask about fees, packing, and where to drop them off (oversize counter vs. check-in).

3) Pack for rough handling

  • Hard cases for instruments; loosen strings slightly.
  • Bubble wrap corners; add “fragile” tags (not magic, but helps).
  • Put your name and number inside the case as well.
Rental hack: For short trips, renting gear on arrival can beat fees + hassle—especially for bulky items.

4) Insurance & declaration

Expensive instruments and pro gear? Check coverage and declare value if the airline offers it.

5) Airport day

  • Arrive earlier (oversize counters can queue).
  • Keep soft straps taped down to avoid conveyor snags.
