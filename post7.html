Seat Selection 101: What’s Worth Paying For

Published Jun 18, 2025 • 5 min read

Not all seats are equal—and not all upgrades are worth the price. Here’s how to choose the right seat for your trip and budget.

Quick wins

Short hops:

Skip paid seats unless you need to sit together. Aisle seats speed up exits.

Red-eyes:

Window seat + neck pillow + eye mask = better sleep odds.

When to pay

  • Exit rows/extra legroom: Tall travelers or laptop work time.
  • Quiet zones/front cabin: Faster service and deplaning.
  • Family seating: Guarantee seats together on busy flights.

Save the cash when…

  • You can check-in right when it opens—free decent seats often appear.
  • The aircraft is lightly loaded (midweek midday flights).

Pro moves

  • Watch seat maps in My Bookings—open seats change as people check in.
  • Bring your own headphones; bulkhead seats can lack under-seat storage.
