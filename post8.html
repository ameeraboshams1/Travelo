Travel Safety: Smarter Passwords & 2FA on the Road

Published Jul 9, 2025 • 5 min read

Public Wi-Fi, lost phones, and rushed logins—travel introduces risk. Secure your accounts in 15 minutes with these simple steps.

1) Use a password manager

One strong master password; unique passwords everywhere else. It’s the biggest security upgrade for the least effort.

2) Turn on 2FA (everywhere)

Prefer authenticator apps over SMS when possible. Add backup codes to your secure notes before you fly.

3) Tidy up devices

Phone: enable biometrics, auto-lock under 60s, find-my-device, and encrypted backups.
Laptop: disk encryption, login password, and browser profiles (work vs. travel).

4) Public Wi-Fi sanity

  • Avoid sensitive logins on open networks.
  • Use your mobile hotspot when in doubt.
  • Always check for the padlock/HTTPS.

5) Email hygiene

  • Archive travel confirmations in a “Trips” label.
  • Beware fake airline alerts; verify sender domain.
