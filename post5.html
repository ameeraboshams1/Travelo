Family Travel: Flying with Infants Without the Meltdown

Published Dec 11, 2024 • 10 min read

Flying with a baby doesn’t have to be chaos. With the right prep and a realistic plan, you can keep everyone comfortable and arrive with your sanity intact. Here’s a field-tested guide from Travelo parents and our support team.

1) Book the golden time

Early morning flights are quieter and less delay-prone. If possible, fly during your baby’s nap window. Use Travelo’s Departure Time filter to lock a schedule that aligns with sleep.

2) Choose seats that help

  • Bassinet rows: Request early for long-haul—limited supply.
  • Aisle seat: Easier diaper runs and soothing strolls.
  • Seat for car seat: If buying a seat for the infant, confirm dimensions and approval labels.
Pro tip: Pack a light muslin blanket to create shade and reduce stimulation.

3) The three-pouch system

  • Health: diapers, wipes, rash cream, disposable bags, fever reducer.
  • Comfort: spare pacifier, soft toy, blanket, change of clothes (for baby and you).
  • Feeding: bottles or formula, burp cloths, bibs.

4) Takeoff/landing feeding

Swallowing relieves ear pressure. Nurse or offer a bottle during takeoff and landing. Breast pumps and baby milk are typically allowed as baby supplies—follow local security rules.

5) Strollers at the gate

Most airlines let you gate-check a stroller for free. Label it clearly and snap a photo before handing over.

6) Diaper strategy

Airplane lavs with changing tables are tight. Bring a foldable changing mat and small trash bags for odor control.

7) Documents checklist

  • Passport/ID; some routes request a birth certificate.
  • Consent letter if traveling solo with the child (country-specific).

Airport sprint checklist

  • Mobile boarding passes downloaded.
  • Enough diapers for delays.
  • Extra outfit for everyone.
